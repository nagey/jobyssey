QA CV from India- differences between Indian and Irish CVs

Often, we see jobseekers who have recently moved from India using Curriculum Vitae that are not re-formatted to suit the Irish market. Irish hiring managers and HR personnel may find them unclear and it can make it difficult to decide if someone is suitable for a phone screen interview or not. Here is a sample CV from India:

C U R R I C U L A M V I T A E

(Name and contact information removed)

Objective:

To pursue a key technical role as software testing professional in a growing organization where I can maximize my skills with a consistent, clear and simple approach to software testing and grow along with the growth of the company.

Professional Summary:

  • 2.1 Years of Experience in Software Testing.
  • Proficient and extensive work experience in Manual and Automation Testing.
  • Hands on experience with test automation tool QTP.
  • Extensive work experience in writing Test cases based on requirements.· Having experience on web based applications and applications involving client server architecture.
  • Thorough knowledge of SDLC and STLC.
  • Knowledge of CMM standards.
  • Experience on different types of Black Box testing viz. Functional and Regression testing.

If this candidate were seeking a job in Ireland, it would be tough to get a foot in the door with this CV because:

  1. Lots of cruft up front- (and by cruft, I mean things targeted more at search engines than humans). The CV starts with an objective, which is fine, but then runs through professional summary, employment summary, educational profile, and technical skills. In this case, these are so keyword-heavy they seem to be mainly for SEO. For Ireland, it's important to get to the information readers want right away, all of this should go at the end of the CV, if its there at all (if a particular certificate or degree is required it can stay at the top, but it doesn't appear to be here).
  2. Employer details lost amid other random stuff, not with work experience- This is a huge formatting difference between CVs in India and CVs in Ireland. In Ireland, the employer you worked for is most important, not the specific clients you dealt with. In fact, not making your employer easy to find can seem evasive or even dishonest to Irish readers.
  3. Work organized into projects- Work should be organized by employer. If you were truly on completely different projects, it can be broken up by project, but if similar skills were involved combine them.
  4. Too much project/product information, not enough responsibilities- A sentence or two to explain the company or product you were working on is sufficient. Most of the details should be about specific things you were doing.
  5. Too many paragraphs- Keep the reading text to a minimum and bullet whenever possible

If this CV were destined for Ireland, here is how I would reformat it:

Name Here

Contact information here

Objective:

To pursue a key technical role as software testing professional in a growing organization where I can maximize my skills with a consistent, clear and simple approach to software testing and grow along with the growth of the company.

Employment Summary:

Employer Name Here (location)

July 2006-present

Software Testing Engineer

Responsible for software testing for products in the financial services and healthcare industries such as (client name here) and (other client).

Duties:

  • Performed manual testing and testing via Quick Test Pro (QTP)
  • Wrote test cases to verify the functionality of applications based on requirements.
  • Involved in preparation of Functional Test Scripts using QTP.
  • Prepared Execution Reports and Defect Reports.
  • (further points from other project- 8-10 total )

Educational Profile: Completed B.E in Electronics and Communication from (college name here) in June 2006 and secured 68.25 %.

Technical Skills: Testing Tools : Quick Test Professional 8.2, Winrunner 8.0 Load runner 7.5.Defect Tracking/Test Management : Test Director 5.0.Operating Systems : Windows 98/2K/NT/XP,UNIX.Internet Technology : VB Script.Languages : C, Basics of C .Database : SQL server.
